About

Hi! I am a researcher interested in developing data driven insights to inform public policy. I currently work as a Research Associate at the Department of Economics and Public Policy, Indian School of Business Hyderabad with Ashwini Chhatre and Avik Sarkar. I also hold a secondary appointment as a Research Assistant at the Department of Real Estate, Wharton School of Business, University of Pennsylvania.

My current research spans across three areas of public policy;

Infrastructure Policy: studying the equity implications of capital investments in large-scale infrastructure on demography, land acquisition and the environment;

designing experiments and policies for promoting cultivation, public distribution and consumption of sustainable millets in regions vulnerable to climate change, and lastly, understanding the potential of unique digital identity in India to inform bureaucratic decision making for food, employment and scholarship welfare schemes.

Previously, I was a Research Associate at the Clarence Stein Institute of Urban and Landscape Studies, Cornell University.

I am an urban planner by training, having pursued my Masters in Regional Planning at AAP, Cornell University and Bachelors of Physical Planning at the School of Planning and Architecture Delhi.
